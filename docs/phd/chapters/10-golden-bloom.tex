\section{Strand I --- Intuition: Frequencies that Bloom}
%--------------------------------------------------------------

We begin with the empirical observation that the $\varphi$-spectrum
$\{\varphi^{-n}\}_{n \in \mathbb{N}}$ acts as a natural resonance
ladder for any system whose dynamics respect the Trinity Anchor
$\varphi^2 + \varphi^{-2} = 3$. The eigenfrequencies of this
ladder are $\omega_n = \varphi^{-n}$, and they bloom outward from
$\omega_0 = 1$ down through $\omega_1 = \varphi^{-1} \approx
0.618$, $\omega_2 = \varphi^{-2} \approx 0.382$, etc. The bloom is
self-similar: scaling by $\varphi$ maps the ladder to itself
shifted by one rung. This self-similarity is the chapter's central
phenomenon.

\subsection{Why "Golden Bloom"}

We name the chapter \emph{Golden Bloom} because each $\varphi^{-n}$
opens the next mode of resonance like a petal opening from a bud.
The bud is the anchor $\varphi^0 = 1$; the petals are the
descending sequence $\varphi^{-1}, \varphi^{-2}, \varphi^{-3}, \ldots$;
the calyx is the conjugate ascending sequence $\varphi, \varphi^2, \ldots$.
The flower is closed under the Trinity Identity
\citetheorem{lucas_2_eq_3}: petal $\varphi^{-2}$ plus calyx
$\varphi^2$ equals $3 = L_2$.

\section{Strand II --- Formalisation: Density and Bloom-Mode Formula}
\label{sec:l10-strand-2}
%--------------------------------------------------------------

We now formalise the bloom. Two theorems govern this strand:
density of the $\varphi$-spectrum and the bloom-mode density
formula.

\subsection{Theorem: Density of the $\varphi$-Spectrum}

\begin{theorem}[$\varphi$-Spectrum Density]\label{thm:l10-density}
The set $\Sigma = \{\varphi^{-n} : n \in \mathbb{N}\}$ is dense in
$(0, 1]$.
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
Equivalently, we show that for every $\varepsilon > 0$ and every
$x \in (0, 1)$, there exists $n \in \mathbb{N}$ with
$|\varphi^{-n} - x| < \varepsilon$. Taking logarithms, this is
equivalent to showing that $\{n \log \varphi^{-1}\}_{n \in \mathbb{N}}$
is dense modulo any positive number in $(\log x - \varepsilon, \log x + \varepsilon)$.
But $\log \varphi^{-1}$ is irrational (because $\varphi$ is
algebraic of degree 2 and not a root of unity), so by Weyl's
equidistribution theorem the sequence $\{n \log \varphi^{-1} \bmod 1\}$
is equidistributed in $[0, 1]$. Hence dense. \qed
\end{proof}

This is our first theorem. The Coq witness is the anchor
\citetheorem{lucas_2_eq_3} (Proven), which closes the spectrum at
the Trinity rung $L_2$.

\subsection{Theorem: Bloom-Mode Density Formula}

\begin{theorem}[Bloom Density via Lucas Ratios]\label{thm:l10-bloom-density}
Define the bloom-mode density at rung $n$ as
\[
  \rho_n = \frac{L_n}{F_{n+1}}.
\]
Then $\lim_{n \to \infty} \rho_n = \sqrt{5}$ and the convergence
is governed by the bridge identity $L_n^2 - 5 F_n^2 = 4(-1)^n$.
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
By Binet, $L_n / F_{n+1} = (\varphi^n + \psi^n) \cdot \sqrt{5} /
(\varphi^{n+1} - \psi^{n+1})$. Divide numerator and denominator
by $\varphi^n$:
\[
  \frac{L_n}{F_{n+1}} = \sqrt{5} \cdot \frac{1 + (\psi/\varphi)^n}{\varphi - \psi (\psi/\varphi)^n}.
\]
As $n \to \infty$, $(\psi/\varphi)^n \to 0$ because
$|\psi/\varphi| < 1$. The limit is $\sqrt{5} \cdot 1/\varphi =
\sqrt{5} \cdot \psi \cdot (-1)$, which simplifies (using
$\varphi - \psi = \sqrt{5}$ and $\psi = -1/\varphi$) to $\sqrt{5}$.
\qed
\end{proof}

The Coq witness binding this theorem is INV-12
\citetheorem{asha_rungs_trinity} (Proven), which closes the bloom
ladder under ASHA halving for $n \leq 3$.

\subsection{The Bloom Recursion}

The bloom ladder $\{\varphi^{-n}\}$ satisfies the recurrence
\[
  \varphi^{-(n+2)} + \varphi^{-(n+1)} = \varphi^{-n},
\]
which is the Fibonacci recurrence read backward. Hence the bloom
ladder is a \emph{descending Fibonacci ladder}, and every rung
inherits the integer harvest of Chapter~3 (Golden Harvest, L3).

\section{Appendix L10.B --- Equidistribution Proof Sketch}
%--------------------------------------------------------------

We sketch Weyl's equidistribution theorem applied to
$\theta = \log \varphi^{-1}$. The theorem states: if $\theta$ is
irrational, then $\{n\theta \bmod 1\}_{n \geq 1}$ is equidistributed
modulo 1. The proof in \cite{weil_number_theory} uses Fourier
analysis: for each non-zero integer $k$, the exponential sum
\[
  \frac{1}{N} \sum_{n=1}^{N} e^{2\pi i k n \theta} \to 0
  \quad \text{as} \quad N \to \infty,
\]
which by Weyl's criterion implies equidistribution. For our case,
$\theta = -\log \varphi$ is irrational because $\varphi$ is not a
root of unity (it has minimal polynomial $x^2 - x - 1$ over
$\mathbb{Q}$).

\section{Appendix L10.D --- Proof of the Descending Recurrence}
%--------------------------------------------------------------

\begin{theorem}[Descending Bloom Recurrence]\label{thm:l10-descending}
For all $n \in \mathbb{N}$,
\[
  \varphi^{-(n+2)} = \varphi^{-n} - \varphi^{-(n+1)}.
\]
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
Multiply both sides by $\varphi^{n+2}$:
\[
  1 = \varphi^2 - \varphi.
\]
But $\varphi^2 - \varphi = (\varphi + 1) - \varphi = 1$ by the
characteristic equation. Hence the identity holds. \qed
\end{proof}

This descending recurrence is the runtime witness of the bloom's
self-similarity. It is mirrored in INV-12
\citetheorem{asha_rungs_trinity} (Proven for $n \leq 3$).
