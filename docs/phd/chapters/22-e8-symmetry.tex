The central empirical claim of this chapter is the E$_8$-uniqueness

the claim that no simple Lie algebra of dimension greater than 248

(a) contain the Standard Model gauge group $SU(3)\times SU(2)\times U(1)$

(b) exhibit a Coxeter element expressible as a $\phi$-power.

\medskip
\noindent\textbf{Primary falsifier (dim\,$>$\,248 simple algebra).}
Exhibit a simple Lie algebra $\mathfrak{g}$ with:
\begin{enumerate}
  \item $\dim(\mathfrak{g}) > 248$, with $\mathfrak{g}$ not one of the
        infinite families $A_n, B_n, C_n, D_n$,
  \item a Coxeter element $h_{\mathfrak{g}}$ satisfying
        $h_{\mathfrak{g}} = \phi^k$ for some $k \in \mathbb{Q}$,
  \item a subalgebra chain containing
        $SU(3)\times SU(2)\times U(1)$ as a subgroup of rank
        $\leq \mathrm{rank}(\mathfrak{g})$.
\end{enumerate}
Such an algebra, verified by a computer-algebra proof (GAP or LiE),
would refute the E$_8$-uniqueness pillar.  The classification of
simple Lie algebras (Cartan, $\sim$1894; Killing, $\sim$1888) shows
that the exceptional algebras $G_2, F_4, E_6, E_7, E_8$ exhaust all
simple exceptional cases, and none with $\dim > 248$ exists in the
classification.  A falsifier therefore requires either (i) an error
in the Cartan--Killing classification, or (ii) a non-classical,
non-exceptional simple algebra outside the Cartan--Killing list.
Either would constitute a theorem-level mathematical discovery as
well as a falsification of this chapter.

\medskip

scales with the $X$-boson mass $m_X \approx \phi^3 m_Z \approx 386\,\text{GeV}$.

falsifier: it falsifies the mass prediction, not the algebraic
uniqueness claim.

Cartan--Killing classification.  This is a mathematical theorem, not
