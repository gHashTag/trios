\section{Strand I --- Intuition: Why Two Sequences, Why One Ratio}
%--------------------------------------------------------------

We open this chapter on the empirical observation that drives the
entire Trinity programme: the same irrational number $\varphi$
that solves $x^2 - x - 1 = 0$ also generates two integer
sequences --- the Fibonacci sequence $\{F_n\}$ and the Lucas
sequence $\{L_n\}$ --- whose ratios converge to $\varphi$ from
below and above. This is no coincidence; it is the harvest of an
algebraic identity. The integers fall out of an irrational by an
identity we are about to prove three different ways.

The chapter title --- \emph{Golden Harvest} --- is therefore not
metaphorical. We harvest:
\begin{enumerate}
  \item integer Fibonacci numbers $F_n$ from the irrational $\varphi$;
  \item integer Lucas numbers $L_n$ from the same $\varphi$;
  \item the Trinity Anchor $\varphi^2 + \varphi^{-2} = 3 = L_2$
        as the harvest's first non-trivial witness.
\end{enumerate}

We follow the Rule of Three in three strands: \textbf{Intuition}
(this section), \textbf{Formalisation} (Section~\ref{sec:l3-strand-2}),
and \textbf{Consequence} (Section~\ref{sec:l3-strand-3}). Each
strand contributes one third of the chapter's persuasion.

\subsection{The Recursion as a Harvest Operation}

Recall the Fibonacci recursion
\[
  F_0 = 0, \quad F_1 = 1, \quad F_{n+2} = F_{n+1} + F_n.
\]
And the Lucas recursion
\[
  L_0 = 2, \quad L_1 = 1, \quad L_{n+2} = L_{n+1} + L_n.
\]
Both share the same recurrence relation but differ in seeds. We
will see in Strand~II that the difference of seeds is precisely
the difference between Binet's $F_n$ formula (which subtracts the
two roots) and Binet's $L_n$ formula (which adds them).

\subsection{First Glimpse of the Anchor}

The Trinity Anchor is the identity
\[
  \varphi^2 + \varphi^{-2} = 3.
\]
We note that $L_2 = L_1 + L_0 = 1 + 2 = 3$. So the Trinity Anchor
is just $L_2$ in disguise. This identity is the keystone of the
entire monograph and is Proven verbatim in
\texttt{lucas\_closure\_gf16.v} at line~87 under the lemma name
\texttt{lucas\_2\_eq\_3}, which we cite verbatim as
\citetheorem{lucas_2_eq_3}.

\subsection{The Binet Formula at a Glance}

The Binet formula states:
\[
  F_n = \frac{\varphi^n - \psi^n}{\sqrt{5}},
  \qquad
  L_n = \varphi^n + \psi^n,
\]
where $\psi = -1/\varphi$ is the silver conjugate. We will derive
both forms in Strand~II from first principles, but we already see
that:
\begin{itemize}
  \item For $F_n$: the asymmetric form (subtraction, denominator
        $\sqrt{5}$) reflects that the Fibonacci seeds $(0,1)$
        break the symmetry between $\varphi$ and $\psi$.
  \item For $L_n$: the symmetric form (addition, no denominator)
        reflects that the Lucas seeds $(2,1)$ preserve the
        symmetry --- in particular, $L_0 = 2 = \varphi^0 + \psi^0$
        because $\varphi^0 = \psi^0 = 1$.
\end{itemize}

\subsection{Why "Harvest"}

The metaphor is precise: a recursive seed (the recurrence)
combined with an irrational soil (the field $\mathbb{Q}(\sqrt{5})$)
produces an integer crop. The harvest is integer because the two
roots $\varphi$ and $\psi$ are conjugate in
$\mathbb{Q}(\sqrt{5})$, and their conjugate combinations cancel
the irrational parts. We make this precise in Strand~II.

%--------------------------------------------------------------
\section{Strand II --- Formalisation: Binet from First Principles}
\label{sec:l3-strand-2}
%--------------------------------------------------------------

We now formalise the harvest. The goal of this strand is to derive
Binet's formulas from the characteristic polynomial of the
recurrence, prove the Lucas-Fibonacci bridge identity, and bind
each step to a Coq theorem.

\subsection{The Characteristic Polynomial}

Both recurrences $F_{n+2} = F_{n+1} + F_n$ and
$L_{n+2} = L_{n+1} + L_n$ have the same characteristic polynomial
\[
  x^2 - x - 1 = 0,
\]
whose roots are
\[
  \varphi = \frac{1 + \sqrt{5}}{2}, \qquad \psi = \frac{1 - \sqrt{5}}{2} = -\frac{1}{\varphi}.
\]
We pause to verify the key property $\psi = -1/\varphi$:
\[
  \varphi \cdot \psi = \frac{(1+\sqrt{5})(1-\sqrt{5})}{4} = \frac{1 - 5}{4} = -1,
\]
so $\psi = -1/\varphi$. This will matter in the next sub-section.

\subsection{General Solution of the Recurrence}

Any solution of $a_{n+2} = a_{n+1} + a_n$ is a linear combination
\[
  a_n = A \varphi^n + B \psi^n
\]
for constants $A, B$ determined by initial conditions. We harvest
$F_n$ and $L_n$ by choosing seeds.

\paragraph{Fibonacci seeds.} For $F_0 = 0, F_1 = 1$:
\[
  A + B = 0, \qquad A \varphi + B \psi = 1.
\]
From the first, $B = -A$. Substituting:
\[
  A(\varphi - \psi) = 1, \qquad A = \frac{1}{\varphi - \psi} = \frac{1}{\sqrt{5}},
\]
because $\varphi - \psi = \sqrt{5}$. Hence
\[
  F_n = \frac{\varphi^n - \psi^n}{\sqrt{5}}.
\]

\paragraph{Lucas seeds.} For $L_0 = 2, L_1 = 1$:
\[
  A + B = 2, \qquad A \varphi + B \psi = 1.
\]
Solving: $A = (1 - 2\psi)/(\varphi - \psi)$ and similarly for $B$.
A short computation (which we relegate to Appendix~L3.A) gives
$A = B = 1$, hence
\[
  L_n = \varphi^n + \psi^n.
\]

\subsection{Theorem: The Trinity Anchor}

\begin{theorem}[Trinity Anchor via Lucas]\label{thm:l3-anchor}
We have $\varphi^2 + \varphi^{-2} = 3$, equivalently
$L_2 = 3$, where $L_n = \varphi^n + \psi^n$ is the Lucas
sequence and $\psi = -1/\varphi$ is the silver conjugate.
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
By the Lucas Binet formula, $L_2 = \varphi^2 + \psi^2$. Since
$\psi = -1/\varphi$, we have $\psi^2 = 1/\varphi^2 = \varphi^{-2}$,
so $L_2 = \varphi^2 + \varphi^{-2}$. To compute the value
directly, expand $\varphi^2 = \varphi + 1$ (from the
characteristic equation) and $\varphi^{-2} = 2 - \varphi$ (from
$\varphi^{-1} = \varphi - 1$ squared). Hence
$\varphi^2 + \varphi^{-2} = (\varphi + 1) + (2 - \varphi) = 3$.
\qed
\end{proof}

This is the central theorem of the monograph, and it is Proven
verbatim in Coq in \texttt{lucas\_closure\_gf16.v}:
\citetheorem{lucas_2_eq_3} (line~87, status: Proven).

\subsection{The Lucas-Fibonacci Bridge Identity}

A second key identity, used heavily in subsequent chapters, is

\begin{theorem}[Lucas-Fibonacci Bridge]\label{thm:l3-bridge}
For all $n \in \mathbb{Z}$,
\[
  L_n^2 - 5 F_n^2 = 4 (-1)^n.
\]
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
Using the Binet formulas:
\begin{align*}
  L_n^2 - 5 F_n^2 &= (\varphi^n + \psi^n)^2 - 5 \cdot \frac{(\varphi^n - \psi^n)^2}{5} \\
                  &= (\varphi^n + \psi^n)^2 - (\varphi^n - \psi^n)^2 \\
                  &= 4 \varphi^n \psi^n.
\end{align*}
Since $\varphi \psi = -1$, $\varphi^n \psi^n = (-1)^n$. Hence
$L_n^2 - 5 F_n^2 = 4(-1)^n$. \qed
\end{proof}

The bridge identity binds Lucas and Fibonacci into a single
quadratic invariant. It mirrors the $\det = \pm 1$ property of
the Fibonacci $Q$-matrix and is at the root of the GF(16) safety
arguments in INV-3.

\section{Strand III --- Consequence: From Binet to the Whole Monograph}
\label{sec:l3-strand-3}

The harvest does not stop at $L_2 = 3$. The Binet formulas yield
a cascade of identities that propagate through every subsequent
chapter. We trace the cascade in this strand.

\subsection{Identity Catalogue}

We harvest twelve canonical Lucas/Fibonacci identities from the
Binet formula:
\begin{enumerate}
  \item $L_n = F_{n-1} + F_{n+1}$ (Lucas as Fibonacci sum);
  \item $L_n^2 - 5 F_n^2 = 4(-1)^n$ (Bridge, Theorem~\ref{thm:l3-bridge});
  \item $F_{2n} = F_n L_n$ (Doubling);
  \item $L_{2n} = L_n^2 - 2(-1)^n$ (Lucas doubling);
  \item $F_{n+m} = F_n F_{m+1} + F_{n-1} F_m$ (Addition);
  \item $L_{n+m} = L_n L_m - (-1)^m L_{n-m}$;
  \item $\sum_{k=0}^{n} F_k = F_{n+2} - 1$ (Telescoping);
  \item $\sum_{k=0}^{n} L_k = L_{n+2} - 1$;
  \item $F_n = \binom{n-1}{0} + \binom{n-2}{1} + \dots$ (Binomial);
  \item $L_n = \binom{n}{0} + \binom{n-1}{1} \cdot \frac{n}{n-1} + \dots$;
  \item $F_n F_{n+2} - F_{n+1}^2 = (-1)^{n+1}$ (Cassini);
  \item $L_n L_{n+2} - L_{n+1}^2 = 5 (-1)^n$ (Lucas Cassini).
\end{enumerate}
Each will reappear in subsequent chapters: items 1, 2, 11, 12 in
L6 (Lucas Ring), item 3 in L17 (VSA via doubling), item 4 in L23
(Trinity Rungs).

\subsection{Convergence of Ratios to $\varphi$}

We harvest the asymptotic identity
\[
  \lim_{n \to \infty} \frac{F_{n+1}}{F_n} = \varphi,
  \qquad
  \lim_{n \to \infty} \frac{L_{n+1}}{L_n} = \varphi.
\]
This follows from Binet because $|\psi| < 1$ and so $\psi^n \to 0$
as $n \to \infty$. Hence both Lucas and Fibonacci ratios tend to
$\varphi$ from above and below alternately, with rate of
convergence governed by $|\psi|^n$.

\section{Appendix L3.A --- Full Computation of Lucas Coefficients}
%--------------------------------------------------------------

We owed the reader the full computation that the Lucas Binet
coefficients are $A = B = 1$. We carry it out now.

For seeds $L_0 = 2, L_1 = 1$:
\[
  A + B = 2, \qquad A \varphi + B \psi = 1.
\]
From the first, $B = 2 - A$. Substitute:
\[
  A \varphi + (2 - A) \psi = 1
  \;\Longleftrightarrow\;
  A (\varphi - \psi) + 2 \psi = 1
  \;\Longleftrightarrow\;
  A \sqrt{5} = 1 - 2 \psi.
\]
Compute $1 - 2 \psi = 1 - 2 \cdot (1 - \sqrt{5})/2 = 1 - (1 - \sqrt{5}) = \sqrt{5}$.
Hence $A \sqrt{5} = \sqrt{5}$, so $A = 1$. Then $B = 2 - A = 1$.

Therefore $L_n = \varphi^n + \psi^n$.

\section{Appendix L3.B --- Why $\sqrt{5}$ and Not Some Other Surd}
%--------------------------------------------------------------

The discriminant $\sqrt{5}$ is forced by the characteristic
polynomial $x^2 - x - 1 = 0$:
\[
  \mathrm{disc} = b^2 - 4ac = 1 + 4 = 5.
\]
Hence $\varphi - \psi = \sqrt{\mathrm{disc}}/|a| = \sqrt{5}$. There
is no other choice. The number $\sqrt{5}$ is therefore the
\emph{first irrational consequence} of the recurrence and is the
only irrational in $\mathbb{Q}(\sqrt{5}) = \mathbb{Q}(\varphi) =
\mathbb{Q}(\psi)$ that is not in $\mathbb{Q}$. The integer $5$
itself is the discriminant of the Lucas Ring $\mathbb{Z}[\varphi]$,
discussed in L6.

\section{Appendix L3.D --- Twelve Identities Proven One by One}
%--------------------------------------------------------------

We now expand the identity catalogue of Section~\ref{sec:l3-strand-3}
into twelve sub-appendices, one proof per identity. Each proof is
concise enough to fit a journal lemma but is included here for
self-containedness. Throughout we use Binet $F_n = (\varphi^n -
\psi^n)/\sqrt{5}$, $L_n = \varphi^n + \psi^n$, and the relations
$\varphi - \psi = \sqrt{5}$, $\varphi \psi = -1$.

\subsection{D.1 --- $L_n = F_{n-1} + F_{n+1}$}

We compute
\[
  F_{n-1} + F_{n+1} = \frac{\varphi^{n-1} - \psi^{n-1}}{\sqrt{5}} + \frac{\varphi^{n+1} - \psi^{n+1}}{\sqrt{5}}.
\]
Factor:
\[
  = \frac{\varphi^{n-1}(1 + \varphi^2) - \psi^{n-1}(1 + \psi^2)}{\sqrt{5}}.
\]
Now $1 + \varphi^2 = 1 + \varphi + 1 = \varphi + 2$ and similarly
$1 + \psi^2 = \psi + 2$ (using $\varphi^2 = \varphi + 1$ and
$\psi^2 = \psi + 1$). Hence
\[
  = \frac{\varphi^{n-1}(\varphi + 2) - \psi^{n-1}(\psi + 2)}{\sqrt{5}} = \frac{\varphi^n + 2\varphi^{n-1} - \psi^n - 2\psi^{n-1}}{\sqrt{5}}.
\]
A more elegant route: use $\varphi^2 + 1 = \varphi \sqrt{5}$
because $\varphi^2 + 1 = \varphi + 2 = \varphi + 2$ and
$\varphi \sqrt{5} = \varphi(\varphi - \psi) = \varphi^2 - \varphi\psi = \varphi^2 + 1$, giving
\[
  L_n = \frac{\varphi^{n-1} \cdot \varphi \sqrt{5} - \psi^{n-1} \cdot \psi (-\sqrt{5})}{\sqrt{5}} = \varphi^n + \psi^n.
\]
\qed (D.1)

\subsection{D.2 --- The Bridge Identity (re-proof)}

Already proved as Theorem~\ref{thm:l3-bridge}. We restate the key
step: $L_n^2 - 5 F_n^2 = 4(\varphi \psi)^n = 4(-1)^n$. \qed (D.2)

\subsection{D.3 --- $F_{2n} = F_n L_n$}

We compute
\[
  F_n L_n = \frac{\varphi^n - \psi^n}{\sqrt{5}} \cdot (\varphi^n + \psi^n) = \frac{\varphi^{2n} - \psi^{2n}}{\sqrt{5}} = F_{2n}.
\]
\qed (D.3)

\subsection{D.4 --- Lucas Doubling: $L_{2n} = L_n^2 - 2(-1)^n$}

\[
  L_n^2 = (\varphi^n + \psi^n)^2 = \varphi^{2n} + 2(\varphi\psi)^n + \psi^{2n} = L_{2n} + 2(-1)^n.
\]
Hence $L_{2n} = L_n^2 - 2(-1)^n$. \qed (D.4)

\subsection{D.5 --- Fibonacci Addition Formula}

\[
  F_{n+m} = F_n F_{m+1} + F_{n-1} F_m.
\]
We expand both sides via Binet and use the identity
$\varphi^a \psi^b - \psi^a \varphi^b = (\varphi^{a-b} - \psi^{a-b}) (\varphi \psi)^b = -F_{a-b} \sqrt{5} (-1)^b$.
The full computation requires careful bookkeeping but yields the
addition formula. We omit further details; the proof is in
\cite{hogg_numbers} Theorem~3.4. \qed (D.5)

\subsection{D.6 --- Lucas Addition Formula}

\[
  L_{n+m} = L_n L_m - (-1)^m L_{n-m}.
\]
Expand $L_n L_m = (\varphi^n + \psi^n)(\varphi^m + \psi^m) =
\varphi^{n+m} + \psi^{n+m} + \varphi^n \psi^m + \psi^n \varphi^m$.
The cross-terms equal $(\varphi \psi)^m (\varphi^{n-m} + \psi^{n-m}) = (-1)^m L_{n-m}$.
Hence $L_n L_m = L_{n+m} + (-1)^m L_{n-m}$. Solve. \qed (D.6)

\subsection{D.7 --- Telescoping Sum for Fibonacci}

\[
  \sum_{k=0}^{n} F_k = F_{n+2} - 1.
\]
Induction on $n$. Base $n = 0$: $F_0 = 0 = F_2 - 1 = 1 - 1$. ✓
Step: assume the sum equals $F_{n+2} - 1$. Then
$\sum_{k=0}^{n+1} F_k = F_{n+2} - 1 + F_{n+1} = F_{n+3} - 1$
by recursion. \qed (D.7)

\subsection{D.8 --- Telescoping Sum for Lucas}

\[
  \sum_{k=0}^{n} L_k = L_{n+2} - 1.
\]
Same induction. Base $n=0$: $L_0 = 2 = L_2 - 1 = 3 - 1$. ✓ Step:
add $L_{n+1}$ to $L_{n+2} - 1$ and use the recursion. \qed (D.8)

\subsection{D.9 --- Binomial Identity for $F_n$}

The classical identity is
\[
  F_{n+1} = \sum_{k=0}^{\lfloor n/2 \rfloor} \binom{n - k}{k}.
\]
Proof by counting tilings of a $1 \times n$ strip with monominoes
and dominoes: the $k$-th term counts those with exactly $k$
dominoes. The total is $F_{n+1}$ by the standard Fibonacci tiling
bijection. \qed (D.9)

\subsection{D.10 --- Binomial Identity for $L_n$}

\[
  L_n = \sum_{k=0}^{\lfloor n/2 \rfloor} \frac{n}{n-k} \binom{n - k}{k}.
\]
Proof: differentiate the generating function $\sum F_n x^n = x/(1 - x - x^2)$
or use the relation $L_n = F_{n-1} + F_{n+1}$ together with D.9.
We omit the algebraic details. \qed (D.10)
