external examiner a self-contained summary of the monograph, together with
explicit pointers into the canonical sources of truth. The examiner is

\(\textsc{ch.~}n\to\textsc{appendix~F}\to\textsc{coq file}\)

\emph{phd-monograph-auditor} R-rule discipline, in particular R5 (honest
\textsc{Admitted}/\textsc{Proven}) and R14 (every numeric anchor cites a
Coq theorem). Where a theorem is mechanised but the empirical bound is not
yet sealed, the entry reads \textsc{Admitted}; we never silently flip a
status.

\section{Abstract Recap}
\label{sec:examiner-abstract}

The monograph \emph{Flos Aureus} establishes a single organising identity,
the \textbf{Trinity Anchor}
\(\varphi^{2}+\varphi^{-2}=3\), as the binding thread between

\section{Limitations and Threats to Validity}
\label{sec:examiner-limitations}

\paragraph{(L1) Statistical lift.}
The two \texttt{Admitted} theorems
(\S\ref{sec:examiner-admitted}) wait on the \texttt{coq-stat} bridge; until
that bridge is mechanised (Phase~II, 2027-2028), the empirical witnesses
of INV-7 and INV-4 are \emph{statistically} but not \emph{formally}
sealed.

\paragraph{(L2) Single-platform reproduction.}
The reproduction budget of \(\le\)30~minutes is calibrated on Apple
Silicon M3 Pro. Linux x86\_64 reproduction is supported but not
benchmarked; on a Hetzner CCX33 (8\,vCPU, 32\,GiB) the budget rises to
\(\le\)45~minutes (LA lane benchmark).

\paragraph{(L3) Survey coverage (L27).}
The related-work survey covers six traditions; we do not claim coverage
of post-2024 quasicrystal physics, where new five-fold symmetry results
appeared in mid-2025 and are not yet integrated.

\paragraph{(L4) Bibliography balance.}
The publisher-balance discipline (R11) is enforced at the level of the
\(\ge\)150 entries in \texttt{bibliography.bib}; the residual \(\le\)20\%
arXiv-only fraction reflects pre-print results that have not yet
appeared in print.

\paragraph{(L5) Witness scripts vs Rust witnesses.}
The original ONE SHOT v2.0 specification names a witness file
\texttt{assertions/witness/defense\_gate.sh}; this conflicts with R1
(Rust/Zig only). The LD lane resolves the conflict by canonicalising the
witness as \texttt{tools/defense\_gate.rs}; the shell file is retained as
a thin call-through to satisfy the spec letter (\(\le\)10~lines, no
business logic).

\section{Appendix to the Examiner Pack — Pre-Defense Self-Critique}
\label{sec:examiner-self-critique}

Per R12 (Lee/GVSU style) we close with a Lee-style self-critique. The
examiner is encouraged to challenge the following points first; they are
the points the auditor lane (\texttt{phd-monograph-auditor}~v1.0) flags
as the most fragile.

\paragraph{Self-critique 1 — single-anchor ontology.}
The monograph elevates \(\varphi^{2}+\varphi^{-2}=3\) to a universal
organising identity. We claim eight independent witnesses, but the
witnesses share a categorical origin (the \(A_5\)~rotation group). A
sceptic may argue that the categorical origin is the \emph{single}
witness, not eight. We answer: each witness is faithful (the categorical
functor is faithful on each), so the count is honest.

\paragraph{Self-critique 2 — empirical scale.}
The IGLA RACE empirical witness uses \(n=5\) seeds. A sceptic may argue
that \(n=5\) is under-powered for Welch~$t$. We answer: the
pre-registration (\#143:4320342032) fixes \(\alpha=0.01\), \(n=3\)
\emph{minimum}, and the champion commit~\texttt{2446855} attains
\(p<0.001\) at \(n=5\). The R7 falsifier triggers if \emph{any one} of
the five seeds yields BPB~\(\ge1.50\); this is an additive (not
multiplicative) falsifier and is therefore conservative.

\paragraph{Self-critique 3 — Lee/GVSU citation style.}
We adopt numeric \([n]\) citations throughout. A sceptic may prefer
author-year. We answer: the Lee/GVSU style is consistent with the
template \(.\texttt{cls}\) supplied by Springer LNM, and it makes
\texttt{\textbackslash citetheorem\{INV-N\}} unambiguous in
\texttt{\textbackslash hyperref}.

\paragraph{Self-critique 4 — R6 boundary.}
The R6 ownership rule forbids cross-edits between chapters and
cross-cuts. A sceptic may observe that the LD lane (this pack) cites
chapter content. We answer: cross-citation is permitted; cross-edit is
forbidden. The pack \emph{reads} chapter content but \emph{writes} only
inside \texttt{docs/phd/defense/}.

\paragraph{Self-critique 5 — single platform.}
See limitation~(L2). The benchmark on Apple Silicon does not generalise
trivially to Linux x86\_64. The LA lane closes this with a Hetzner
benchmark.
