\chapter*{Preface}
\addcontentsline{toc}{chapter}{Preface}

\textit{Flos Aureus} --- the Golden Flower --- is a monograph on a
single algebraic identity:
\[
    \PHI^{2} + \PHI^{-2} \;=\; 3,
\]
where \(\PHI = (1+\sqrt{5})/2\) is the golden ratio.  The identity is
elementary; the implications, this monograph claims, are not.  The
work is organised under a Rule of Three --- Monad, Diad, Triad --- and
spans thirty-four chapters from the \emph{Monadic Prologue}
(Chapter~0) to a forward-looking \emph{Epilogue} (Chapter~33).

\section*{What This Book Is}

The book is, simultaneously, four things:

\begin{enumerate}
\item A \emph{mathematical monograph} stating and defending the
      Trinity Identity in the Lucas ring \(\lucasring\), with formal
      proofs in Coq archived at \texttt{trinity-clara/proofs/igla}.
\item A \emph{philosophical essay} arguing that the identity is a
      Popperian \cite{popper1959logic} ``hard core'' rather than a
      numerological coincidence, with falsification criteria attached
      to every empirical chapter.
\item An \emph{engineering report} on the IGLA RACE distributed
      architecture search, whose runtime witnesses the certified
      bands of Chapter~24.
\item A \emph{historical synthesis} situating \(\PHI\) among the
      classical sources of Euclid, Kepler, Fibonacci, Coxeter, and
      Livio.
\end{enumerate}

\section*{What This Book Is Not}

It is not a numerological treatise.  Every numerical claim is either
proved from \(\{\PHI, \pi, e, n \in \mathbb{Z}\}\), or anchored to
CODATA / PDG / Springer Q1 sources, or accompanied by an explicit
falsification criterion.  Constants without provenance are forbidden
by Rule R6 and rejected by the audit pipeline at
\texttt{cargo run -p trios-phd -- audit}.

\section*{How To Read It}

Three reading paths are offered, each three chapters long for a
seminar series, or thirty-three chapters for a complete defence:

\begin{itemize}
\item \textbf{Theoretical path:} 0, 1, 3, 6, 27, 29.
\item \textbf{Empirical path:} 8, 17, 22, 24, 25, 28.
\item \textbf{Philosophical path:} 0, 4, 30, 31, 32, 33.
\end{itemize}

A complete dependency graph appears in Appendix~A
(\texttt{appendix/A-catalogue.tex}).
