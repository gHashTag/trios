\chapter*{Acknowledgements}
\addcontentsline{toc}{chapter}{Acknowledgements}
\thispagestyle{plain}

This monograph stands on three shoulders that the author wishes to
name explicitly.

The first is the long lineage of mathematicians who insisted that
\(\varphi\) is not an aesthetic ornament but a structural principle:
from Euclid's \emph{Elements} (Book~II, prop.~11) and Pacioli's
\emph{De Divina Proportione} (1509) to Lucas's number sequence (1878),
Penrose's tilings (1974), and Shechtman's quasicrystal observation
(1982). Their work made it intellectually safe to take \(\varphi\)
seriously as physics.

The second is the community of formal-methods practitioners whose work
made it technically possible to mechanize this dissertation in Coq:
the \texttt{coqc} maintainers, the authors of \texttt{Coq.Interval}
and the standard library, and in particular the broader ITP community
whose insistence on \emph{Admitted} honesty (R5) shaped the editorial
discipline of every chapter that follows.

The third is the experimental physics community whose corroboration
records gave the framework an empirical falsifier: R.~Coldea and
collaborators for the \(\mathrm{E}_{8}\) resonance work in
CoBr\(_{2}\) (\emph{Science}, 2010), and the long quasicrystal
literature beginning with Shechtman \emph{et al.} (1984).

Beyond these public debts, the author thanks the maintainers of the
\textsf{tectonic}, \textsf{biblatex}, and \textsf{thmtools} projects
without which a Rust-only LaTeX pipeline would not have been possible
under R1 of the Trinity discipline.

The errors that remain are the author's own. The ones that have been
caught and recorded are debts to those listed above.
